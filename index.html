Understanding the Data

Table

1. Image: Screenshot of the tested strategy on TradingView

2. Status: Indicates whether the strategy is currently Profitable or Not Profitable based on testing results.

3. On Bybit: Indicators located on bybit.

4. Indicator(s): This column lists the technical indicators used in the strategy.

5. Type of strategy: This column identifies the type of strategy.

6. Trading Time Frame: This column indicates the chart timeframe used for the strategy.

7. Testing Duration: This column indicates the total time period over which the strategy was tested.

8. Number of Trades: This column shows the total number of trades executed during the testing period.

9. TP/SL: This column specifies the Take Profit (TP) and Stop Loss (SL) levels used in the strategy, defining the profit target and risk limit for each trade.

Cross: This indicates that Take Profit (TP) and Stop Loss (SL) are triggered based on the crossing of specific indicator levels or signals, rather than fixed price points.

10. Leverage: This column specifies the level of leverage used in the strategy, indicating how much the trading position is amplified relative to the account balance.

11. Strategy success (%): This column represents the percentage of trades that were profitable during the testing period, indicating the strategy's success rate.

12. Inspired by: Indicates whether the strategy was based on external inspiration, such as a YouTube video (CLICK HERE), or if it was completely self-created ("SELF MADE").
